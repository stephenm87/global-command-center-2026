Structured Analysis Scaffolding

The 5W1H framework provides students with a consistent analytical structure across all events. Each dimension is auto-generated from the event's entity, key players, timeline, geographic coordinates, and category data.

This mirrors the case library format in the GloPo Companion, creating a unified learning experience across both platforms.

Pedagogical Value

Students learn to decompose complex geopolitical events into their component parts before attempting theoretical analysis. The WHO identifies actors and power dynamics; the WHY connects to structural causes; the HOW reveals mechanisms of action.

HL Global Challenges

Category-Mapped Challenge Analysis

Events in "Geopolitics and Conflict" map to Security, Borders, and Identity challenges. "Economy and Trade" events map to Poverty, Equality, and Environment. Each mapping includes a paragraph of contextualized analysis specific to that event.

This helps Higher Level students connect real-world developments to the IB curriculum's seven global challenges: Security, Borders, Identity, Technology, Environment, Health, and Poverty.

Consistent Analytical Framework

The 5W1H structure (Who, What, Where, When, Why, How) provides students with a repeatable analytical method. Each case includes:

Primary and secondary actors (WHO), the specific issue or dispute (WHAT), geographic context (WHERE), timeline milestones (WHEN), underlying drivers (WHY), and mechanisms of action (HOW).

Cross-Platform Alignment

The same 5W1H framework appears in the Global Command Center's live event modals, ensuring students encounter a consistent analytical approach whether studying curated cases or analyzing breaking news.
